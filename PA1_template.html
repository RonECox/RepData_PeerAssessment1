What is the impact of imputing missing data on the estimates of the total daily number of steps?
Imputing the data has a tendency to change the statistical characteristics of the data set (e.g., change the mean and quantile breakpoints).

More importantly, the null values resulted from full days without activity recorded rather than sporadic null values throughout a day at various 5-minute intervals; therefore, it would have been more prudent to simply eliminate the incomplete cases instead of imputing values into previously unrecorded days.
